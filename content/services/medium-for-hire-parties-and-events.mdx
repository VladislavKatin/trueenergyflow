## Overview

Medium for Hire: Parties and Events is designed for hosts who want a thoughtful spiritual element for a gathering without turning the room into a spectacle. The tone is calm, respectful, and clearly structured. This offering can fit private parties, women's circles, small retreats, celebration weekends, and intimate client events where guests want meaningful connection rather than stage-style performance.

The experience is built around emotional safety, realistic expectations, and a clear container. That means no guarantees, no pressure on guests to participate, and no medical or legal claims. Instead, the focus stays on supportive insight, reflection, and a memorable shared experience.

If you are comparing event-based mediumship with a private session, read [how to book a medium for hire for parties and private events](/blog/how-to-book-a-medium-for-hire-for-parties-and-private-events).

## Who this service fits best

This service often works well for:

- private hosts planning intentional gatherings
- retreat facilitators who want a spiritual guest experience
- small celebrations with a reflective tone
- communities that prefer a warm, conversational format over a theatrical one
- event planners who want clear boundaries and professionalism

The strongest fit is usually a group that values consent, pacing, and emotional maturity.

## Session emphasis

Depending on the event, the format may include:

- an opening grounding practice
- shared intuitive or mediumship messages for the group
- optional mini-readings within a structured time block
- closing integration guidance for guests

Every event is shaped around the group size, schedule, privacy needs, and overall atmosphere.

## Practical event structure

A typical event planning flow includes:

1. **Pre-event coordination**  
   Clarify audience size, venue, timing, and boundaries.

2. **Expectation setting**  
   Guests receive simple language about what the experience is and is not.

3. **Live session facilitation**  
   The event is guided with pacing, consent, and room awareness.

4. **Closing and transition**  
   Guests are brought back to the room in a steady, grounded way.

## What hosts should know

Good event mediumship is not about forcing contact or making everyone react dramatically. It is about creating a respectful environment where people can stay present, open, and discerning. Hosts should plan enough space around the event so guests are not rushed in and out.

For etiquette guidance, see [medium for hire event etiquette: what hosts should know](/blog/medium-for-hire-event-etiquette-what-hosts-should-know).

## Scope and safety

This offering is spiritual and educational. It is not therapy, not medical advice, and not a substitute for crisis care, diagnosis, or legal guidance. Participation should always remain optional, and guests are encouraged to use personal discernment.

## FAQ

### Is this only for large parties?

No. Smaller gatherings are often the best fit because they allow for a calmer and more personal experience.

### Will every guest receive an individual message?

Not necessarily. The format depends on time, group size, and what is agreed in advance.

### Can this be booked for retreats or private community events?

Yes. Retreats, circles, and intimate events are common use cases for this service.
